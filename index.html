  • Identification of human activity (1): pollution / tourism / overfishing / coastal development
  • Development of how it threatens (1): e.g. pollution kills marine life / tourism damages coral through physical contact

Example: Tourism (1) can damage coral reefs when tourists step on or touch them, breaking the delicate structures and preventing reef growth (1).

Explain one type of hard engineering that is used in coastal management.

✓ Mark Scheme

  • Identification (1): sea wall / groyne / rock armour / gabions
  • Explanation of how it works (1): e.g. sea walls reflect wave energy / groynes trap sediment from longshore drift

Example: Groynes (1) are barriers built perpendicular to the coast that trap sediment transported by longshore drift, building up the beach which protects the coast (1).

📌 3-MARK QUESTIONS
3M May 2023
Explain the process of longshore drift.

✓ Mark Scheme (Award up to 3 marks)

  • Waves approach the beach at an angle (1)
  • Swash carries material up the beach at the same angle (1)
  • Backwash carries material straight down due to gravity (1)
  • Results in zig-zag movement of sediment along the coast (1)

4M May 2023
Figure 2a: Diagram showing concordant coastline (rock layers parallel to coast) and discordant coastline (rock layers perpendicular creating headlands and bays)
Explain two factors that could influence landforms on this coastline.

✓ Mark Scheme (2 marks per factor)

Possible factors:

  • Geology/rock type (1) - harder rocks resist erosion forming headlands while softer rocks erode to form bays (1)
  • Wave energy (1) - high energy waves cause more erosion creating cliffs and wave-cut platforms (1)
  • Coastal orientation (1) - angle coastline faces affects wave approach and erosion patterns (1)
4M May 2023
Explain one advantage and one disadvantage of soft engineering strategies.

✓ Mark Scheme (2 + 2 = 4 marks)

Advantages (2 marks):

  • Cheaper than hard engineering (1) so more cost-effective for long stretches (1)
  • More natural/sustainable (1) works with natural processes (1)
  • Maintains ecosystems (1) supporting wildlife habitats like sand dunes (1)

Disadvantages (2 marks):

  • Less effective protection (1) doesn't provide solid barrier against waves (1)
  • Requires ongoing maintenance (1) beach nourishment needs regular replenishment (1)
4M Nov 2021
Figure 2a: World map showing coral reefs concentrated in tropical waters between tropics, particularly Caribbean, Great Barrier Reef, Southeast Asia, Red Sea. Absent from eastern coasts and high latitudes.
Suggest two physical factors that affect the distribution of coral reef ecosystems.

✓ Mark Scheme (2 marks per factor)

  • Water temperature (1) - coral needs warm water 23-29°C, found in tropical areas (1)
  • Sunlight/light penetration (1) - coral needs shallow clear water <25m for photosynthesis (1)
  • Salinity (1) - coral requires full salinity seawater, can't survive near river mouths (1)
  • Water clarity (1) - needs clear water free from sediment for sunlight (1)
4M Nov 2021
Explain two ways the use of predictive techniques could reduce the impact of coastal flooding.

✓ Mark Scheme (2 marks per way)

  • Weather forecasting/storm tracking (1) allows authorities to warn people to evacuate to safety (1)
  • Tide prediction models (1) identify when storm surges coincide with high tides, enabling emergency prep (1)
  • Flood risk mapping (1) identifies vulnerable areas allowing better defense planning (1)
  • Early warning systems (1) give communities time to move valuables and board properties (1)

Figure 2d: Katrina 2005: 1,000 deaths, 200,000 homes destroyed, 50 levees failed | Post-2005: $14bn defenses built | Ida 2021: No levee failures | Warning: Protection "may only last a few more years"
Analyse the effectiveness of the coastal flood prevention strategies shown. Refer to the resources in your answer.

Level 3 (7-8): Thorough analysis with clear evaluation. Effective use of both figures. Balanced assessment. Clear judgment.

Level 2 (4-6): Clear analysis with some evaluation. Uses figure evidence. May lack full development.

Level 1 (1-3): Simple descriptive statements. Limited use of figures.

Key points:

  • Effectiveness: Hurricane Ida 2021 - no levee failures vs Katrina 2005 - 50 failed

  • Ongoing risk: Large areas still at high/medium risk despite defenses
