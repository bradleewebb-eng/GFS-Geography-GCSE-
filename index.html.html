2M May 2023 Paper 1 Q2c
Explain one way human activity can threaten coastal ecosystems. (2 marks)

Mark Scheme:

  • Identification of human activity (1), e.g., pollution / tourism / overfishing / coastal development
  • Development of how it threatens ecosystem (1), e.g., pollution kills marine life / tourism damages coral reefs through physical contact

Example full answer: Tourism (1) can damage coral reefs when tourists step on or touch them, breaking the delicate coral structures and preventing reef growth (1).

2M November 2021 Paper 1 Q2b(iii)
Explain one type of hard engineering that is used in coastal management. (2 marks)

Mark Scheme:

  • Identification of hard engineering strategy (1), e.g., sea wall / groyne / rock armour / gabions
  • Explanation of how it works (1), e.g., sea walls reflect wave energy back out to sea / groynes trap sediment being moved by longshore drift

Example full answer: Groynes (1) are wooden or concrete barriers built perpendicular to the coast that trap sediment being transported by longshore drift, building up the beach which protects the coast (1).

📌 3-MARK QUESTIONS
3M May 2023 Paper 1 Q2e
Explain the process of longshore drift. (3 marks)

Mark Scheme:

  • Waves approach the beach at an angle (1)
  • Swash carries material up the beach at the same angle (1)
  • Backwash carries material straight down the beach due to gravity (1)
  • This results in the zig-zag movement of sediment along the coast (1)

Note: Award up to 3 marks for a clear sequenced explanation

📌 4-MARK QUESTIONS
4M May 2023 Paper 1 Q2d
Study Figure 2a in the Resource Booklet: Diagram showing two types of coastline - a concordant coastline (where rock layers run parallel to the coast creating a smooth coastline) and a discordant coastline (where rock layers run perpendicular to the coast creating alternating headlands and bays).
Explain two factors that could influence landforms on this coastline. (4 marks)

Mark Scheme:

2 marks per factor explained (total 4 marks)

Possible factors:

  • Geology/rock type (1) - harder rocks resist erosion forming headlands while softer rocks erode to form bays (1)
  • Wave energy/power (1) - high energy waves cause more erosion creating features like cliffs and wave-cut platforms (1)
  • Coastal orientation (1) - the angle coastline faces affects wave approach and erosion patterns (1)
  • Concordant/discordant coastline (1) - affects whether headlands and bays form based on geology orientation (1)

Level Descriptors for 4-Mark Questions:

  • Level 2 (3-4 marks): Clear explanation of TWO factors with good development. Shows understanding of coastal processes.
  • Level 1 (1-2 marks): Simple statements about one or two factors with limited development.
  • 0 marks: No relevant content.
4M May 2023 Paper 1 Q2g
Explain one advantage and one disadvantage of soft engineering strategies. (4 marks)

Mark Scheme:

2 marks for advantage + 2 marks for disadvantage = 4 marks total

Advantages (2 marks):

  • Cheaper than hard engineering (1) so more cost-effective for long stretches of coastline (1)
  • More natural/sustainable (1) as it works with natural processes rather than against them (1)
  • Maintains natural coastal ecosystems (1) supporting wildlife habitats like sand dunes (1)

Disadvantages (2 marks):

  • Less effective protection (1) as they don't provide a solid barrier against waves (1)
  • Requires ongoing maintenance (1) such as beach nourishment which needs regular replenishment (1)
  • Takes time to be effective (1) as dune regeneration can take years to provide protection (1)

Level Descriptors for 4-Mark Questions:

  • Level 2 (3-4 marks): Clear explanation of one advantage AND one disadvantage with development.
  • Level 1 (1-2 marks): Simple statements about advantage or disadvantage with limited development.
  • 0 marks: No relevant content.
4M November 2021 Paper 1 Q2c
Study Figure 2a in the Resource Booklet: World map showing the distribution of coral reefs. Reefs are concentrated in tropical waters between the Tropic of Cancer and Tropic of Capricorn, particularly in the Caribbean, Great Barrier Reef (Australia), Southeast Asia, and the Red Sea. They are notably absent from the eastern coasts of continents and high latitude areas.
Suggest two physical factors that affect the distribution of coral reef ecosystems. (4 marks)

Mark Scheme:

2 marks per factor explained (total 4 marks)

  • Water temperature (1) - coral reefs need warm water between 23-29°C, which is why they're found in tropical areas (1)
  • Sunlight/light penetration (1) - coral needs shallow, clear water (less than 25m deep) for photosynthesis by zooxanthellae (1)
  • Salinity (1) - coral requires full salinity seawater and cannot survive near river mouths with freshwater (1)
  • Water clarity (1) - coral needs clear water free from sediment for sunlight to reach symbiotic algae (1)
  • Wave action (1) - some wave action is needed to bring nutrients and oxygen but too much damages coral (1)

Level Descriptors for 4-Mark Questions:

  • Level 2 (3-4 marks): Clear explanation of TWO factors with development. Uses figure evidence effectively.
  • Level 1 (1-2 marks): Simple statements about factors with limited development.
  • 0 marks: No relevant content.
4M November 2021 Paper 1 Q2f
Explain two ways the use of predictive techniques could reduce the impact of coastal flooding. (4 marks)

Mark Scheme:

2 marks per way explained (total 4 marks)

  • Weather forecasting/storm tracking (1) allows authorities to warn people in advance so they can evacuate to safety (1)
  • Tide prediction models (1) help identify when storm surges will coincide with high tides, enabling emergency services to prepare (1)
  • Flood risk mapping (1) identifies vulnerable areas allowing better planning of defenses and evacuation routes (1)
  • Early warning systems (1) give communities time to move valuables and board up properties, reducing damage (1)

Level Descriptors for 4-Mark Questions:

  • Level 2 (3-4 marks): Clear explanation of TWO ways with development showing how impacts are reduced.
  • Level 1 (1-2 marks): Simple statements about predictive techniques with limited development.
  • 0 marks: No relevant content.
📌 8-MARK QUESTIONS
8M May 2023 Paper 1 Q2h
Study Figure 2c and Figure 2d in the Resource Booklet:

Figure 2c: Map of New Orleans showing coastal flood risk zones - large areas colored in red (high risk), orange (medium risk), and yellow (low risk) around the Gulf of Mexico coastline.

Figure 2d: Information about flood prevention in New Orleans, USA:
• Hurricane Katrina (2005): 1,000 deaths, 200,000 homes destroyed, 800,000 evacuated, 50 levees failed
• Post-Katrina improvements: $14 billion network of levees and flood walls built (completed 2012-2018), including flood walls, water pump stations, and flood gates
• Hurricane Ida (2021): No levee or flood wall broken/flooded
• Warning: Protection "may only last a few more years" due to rising sea levels and shrinking levees
Analyse the effectiveness of the coastal flood prevention strategies shown. Refer to the resources in your answer. (8 marks)

Mark Scheme:

Level 3 (7-8 marks): Thorough analysis with clear evaluation of effectiveness. Effective use of both figures. Balanced assessment of successes and limitations. Clear judgment supported by evidence.

Level 2 (4-6 marks): Clear analysis with some evaluation. Uses figure evidence. Shows understanding but may lack full development or balance.

Level 1 (1-3 marks): Simple descriptive statements. Limited use of figures. Basic analysis with little evaluation.

Indicative Content:

  • Evidence of effectiveness: During Hurricane Ida 2021, no levee or flood wall was broken/flooded (Figure 2d) showing the $14 billion investment worked
  • Comparison to past: Contrast with Hurricane Katrina 2005 when 50 levees failed, 1,000 died, 200,000 homes destroyed
  • Range of strategies: Flood walls (completed 2018), water pump stations (2017), flood gates (2012) provide multiple layers of protection
  • Limitations: Figure 2d states protection "may only last a few more years" due to rising sea levels and shrinking levees
  • Ongoing vulnerability: Figure 2c shows large areas still at high/medium flood risk despite defenses
  • Cost considerations: $14 billion investment is significant - is this sustainable long-term?
  • Long-term effectiveness: Hard engineering requires constant maintenance and may become obsolete with climate change

Level Descriptors for 8-Mark Questions:

  • Level 3 (7-8 marks): Thorough and detailed analysis demonstrating comprehensive understanding. Clear structure with developed evaluation. Effective use of both figures and own knowledge. Clear judgment with supported conclusions. Balanced consideration of effectiveness.
  • Level 2 (4-6 marks): Clear analysis with some evaluation. Uses figure evidence. Shows geographical understanding but may lack full development or balance. Some assessment of effectiveness attempted.
  • Level 1 (1-3 marks): Simple, descriptive statements with limited analysis. Basic use of figures. Little evaluation of effectiveness. May be list-like.
  • 0 marks: No relevant content.

Examiner Comments:

Strong responses made clear judgments about effectiveness by comparing the outcomes of Hurricane Katrina (before improvements) with Hurricane Ida (after improvements). They used specific data from the figures such as the $14 billion cost and the zero levee failures in 2021.

Common errors: Just describing the strategies without analyzing effectiveness; ignoring the limitations mentioned in Figure 2d; not using specific data from the figures; failing to reach a clear conclusion about overall effectiveness.

8M November 2021 Paper 1 Q2g
Study Figure 2c in the Resource Booklet: Map and stakeholder views on coastal management along the Dorset coast, UK

Map shows:
• "Hold the line" zones (mainly hard engineering) - Bournemouth, Poole, Swanage
• "Managed retreat" zones
• "Do nothing" zones
• National nature reserve (sand dunes)

Stakeholder quotes:
• Local business owner: "We need the groynes and sea walls; our coastline attracts over 25 million day visitors annually."
• Conservationist: "We just need to let nature take its course and conserve the land to protect species diversity."
• Local councillor: "We recognise the need to protect natural beauty and biodiversity, but also protect residents and livelihoods as tourism is a major employer."
• Tourist: "The sea walls and groynes ruin the scenery a bit... we prefer that the area is just left alone."
Analyse why there may be conflicts over management strategies in coastal environments. (8 marks)

Mark Scheme:

Level 3 (7-8 marks): Thorough analysis of conflicts with clear explanations of why different stakeholders have different views. Effective use of Figure 2c quotes and map. Well-balanced consideration of multiple perspectives.

Level 2 (4-6 marks): Clear explanation of conflicts with some analysis. Uses figure evidence. Shows understanding of different viewpoints but may be one-sided.

Level 1 (1-3 marks): Simple statements about conflicts. Limited use of figure. Basic understanding shown.

Indicative Content:

  • Economic vs environmental: Local business owner wants groynes/sea walls to protect tourism (25 million visitors annually) vs conservationist wants to protect biodiversity
  • Cost of protection: Hard engineering is expensive - conflict over who pays and whether worth it
  • Visual impact: Tourists say "sea walls and groynes ruin the scenery" vs business owner says they're necessary
  • Different management approaches: Map shows "hold the line" in some areas vs "do nothing" vs "managed retreat" - creates winners and losers
  • Local councillor's dilemma: Must balance protecting "natural beauty and biodiversity" with protecting "residents and their livelihood"
  • Short-term vs long-term: Immediate protection vs sustainable approach
  • Protected areas: National nature reserve (sand dunes) means restrictions on development/management

Level Descriptors for 8-Mark Questions:

  • Level 3 (7-8 marks): Thorough analysis showing comprehensive understanding of conflicts. Clear structure with multiple stakeholder perspectives. Effective use of figure quotes. Clear synthesis of different viewpoints.
  • Level 2 (4-6 marks): Clear explanation of conflicts with some analysis. Uses figure evidence. Shows understanding but may focus on only 2-3 perspectives.
  • Level 1 (1-3 marks): Simple statements about disagreements. Limited use of figure. May just list different views without explaining why conflicts arise.
  • 0 marks: No relevant content.

Examiner Comments:

The best responses used direct quotes from Figure 2c and explained WHY each stakeholder had their particular view (e.g., business owner depends on tourism, conservationist prioritizes biodiversity). They also linked different management strategies shown on the map to explain how some areas are protected while others aren't.

Common errors: Just describing what each person said without analyzing the underlying reasons for conflict; not using the map to show how different strategies create winners/losers; failing to explain the economic vs environmental tension clearly.
